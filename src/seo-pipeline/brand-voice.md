# Zion SEO Brand Voice & Content Rules

## Who's reading this

Write primarily for Shopify merchants, ecommerce founders, store managers, and marketers who want to improve their store’s organic visibility.

Most readers will fit one of these situations:

- A new Shopify merchant learning SEO for the first time.
- A store owner whose products are not ranking or being indexed.
- A merchant managing a large catalog and looking for a faster SEO workflow.
- An ecommerce marketer improving product pages, collections, metadata, and content.
- A store owner trying to understand AI search, Shopify `agents.md`, and product discovery through AI assistants.

Write each post for one clear reader and problem. Do not write for a vague audience such as “all ecommerce businesses.”

## Voice

- Direct, practical, and knowledgeable without sounding corporate.
- Explain Shopify SEO in plain language before introducing technical terms.
- Use short sentences and varied paragraph lengths.
- Prefer concrete examples from realistic Shopify stores.
- Be honest about limitations and uncertainty.
- It is acceptable to say that a tactic may not work for every store.
- Avoid exaggerated SaaS language such as “unlock,” “revolutionize,” “skyrocket,” “dominate,” “game-changing,” and “seamless.”
- Do not present SEO as an instant or guaranteed result.
- Do not imply that using Zion SEO alone guarantees rankings, traffic, sales, AI citations, or recommendations.
- Distinguish clearly between established SEO practices, Shopify-specific behavior, and emerging AI-search theories.

## Content principles

Every post should help the reader complete a task, diagnose a problem, or make a better SEO decision.

Good content should answer questions such as:

- What should the merchant change?
- Where should they change it in Shopify?
- Why does it matter?
- How can they check whether it worked?
- What are the risks or limitations?
- Can the task be completed manually, with Shopify, or with an app?

Avoid content that only defines a term without helping the merchant apply it.

## Structure every post must follow

### 1. Opening paragraph

Start with the actual Shopify problem.

Do not begin with broad introductions such as:

- “In today’s competitive ecommerce landscape...”
- “SEO is more important than ever...”
- “Running an online store can be challenging...”

Establish the problem, consequence, and article purpose within 2–4 sentences.

### 2. Quick Answer blockquote

Place a 40–60 word blockquote immediately after the opening paragraph.

It must directly answer the main question implied by the target keyword.

The answer should:

- Stand alone without surrounding context.
- Be specific enough to be useful.
- Avoid promotional language.
- Avoid using “it depends” as the entire answer.
- Mention important limitations when necessary.

### 3. Question-based H2 headings

Every H2 should be phrased as a real question a Shopify merchant might search or ask an AI assistant.

Use:

- “How do you optimize a Shopify product page for SEO?”
- “Why are Shopify products not appearing on Google?”
- “Should product pages and collection pages target the same keyword?”

Avoid:

- “Product Page Optimization”
- “Important SEO Factors”
- “Best Practices”
- “Final Considerations”

Use H3 headings for steps, examples, comparisons, or subtopics within a larger question.

### 4. Practical Shopify instructions

When explaining a task, include actionable Shopify-specific guidance.

Where relevant, mention:

- The Shopify admin section to open.
- The field or setting to edit.
- What the merchant should enter.
- What they should avoid.
- How to preview or verify the change.

Do not invent Shopify settings, menu labels, APIs, theme behavior, or platform limitations. Verify uncertain details before including them.

### 5. Examples

Use realistic ecommerce examples to explain abstract SEO advice.

Good example categories include:

- Clothing.
- Skincare.
- Jewelry.
- Home decor.
- Electronics.
- Supplements.
- Pet products.
- Sports equipment.

Examples must be clearly presented as examples, not real performance data or customer results.

### 6. Internal links

Add internal links naturally within the body.

Link only to real Zion SEO pages listed in the pipeline configuration.

Use descriptive anchor text such as:

- “optimize Shopify product metadata”
- “generate SEO-friendly product descriptions”
- “learn how Zion SEO works”

Never use generic anchor text such as “click here,” “read more,” or “this page.”

Do not invent feature URLs.

### 7. FAQ section

Include at least 6 FAQ questions at the bottom of every article.

Each answer must:

- Be 2–4 sentences.
- Answer the question directly.
- Stand alone without requiring the rest of the article.
- Use real questions discovered through OpenSEO, SERP results, or People Also Ask data.
- Avoid filler questions created only to reach the minimum.

The FAQ content must also be emitted as `FAQPage` structured data using the format expected by the blog pipeline.

### 8. Zion SEO mention

Mention Zion SEO naturally once when it is relevant to the task.

Examples:

- Automating product metadata.
- Generating or improving product descriptions.
- Researching keywords.
- Optimizing product content in bulk.
- Adding richer AI-discovery context.

Do not force a Zion mention into every section.

For broad educational content, one soft mention near the end is enough.

Never imply that Zion provides a feature unless it currently exists.

The blog should establish authority across Shopify SEO as a whole, not only Zion’s product features.

Content may cover:

- Shopify SEO fundamentals.
- Product-page SEO.
- Collection-page SEO.
- Homepage SEO.
- Keyword research.
- Search intent.
- Product descriptions.
- Meta titles and descriptions.
- Internal linking.
- Duplicate content.
- Canonical URLs.
- Indexing problems.
- Sitemaps.
- Robots.txt.
- Structured data.
- Image SEO.
- Alt text.
- Page speed.
- Core Web Vitals.
- Blogging and content strategy.
- Organic traffic growth.
- SEO audits.
- Ecommerce SEO workflows.
- Shopify SEO apps and tools.
- AI search, AEO, GEO, `agents.md`, and AI shopping discovery.

Do not over-focus the entire blog on AI search or product-description generation.

## AI search and AI discovery rules

Treat AI search as an emerging area.

When discussing ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, AI shopping assistants, GEO, AEO, `llms.txt`, or `agents.md`:

- Clearly separate confirmed platform behavior from speculation.
- Prefer official Shopify documentation and primary sources.
- Do not claim that an `agents.md` or `llms.txt` file guarantees citations or recommendations.
- Do not claim that AI assistants always read or use these files.
- Do not promise that structured brand context will directly improve rankings.
- Explain that AI discovery depends on broader factors, including accessible content, product data, authority, relevance, and platform-specific retrieval systems.
- Describe Zion’s AI Discovery Optimizer as adding richer brand and shopping context, not as a guaranteed AI-ranking tool.

## Competitor and comparison content

Comparison posts must be fair and evidence-based.

Before publishing:

- Check the competitor’s current pricing.
- Check its current Shopify App Store listing.
- Verify its advertised features.
- Note when information was last checked.
- Distinguish free features from paid features.
- Do not claim that Zion is better in every category.
- Include situations where the competitor may be a better fit.
- Avoid hostile or dismissive language.

Titles such as “Zion SEO vs [Competitor]” should only be created when there is enough verified information for a useful comparison.

## Case studies and results

Never present hypothetical results as a real case study.

A real case study must identify:

- The original problem.
- The changes made.
- The timeframe.
- The measurement source.
- The result.
- Important external factors.
- Limitations.

When real data is unavailable, label the content clearly as:

- Example.
- Walkthrough.
- Hypothetical scenario.
- Sample workflow.

Do not use invented traffic growth, ranking improvements, conversion increases, or revenue results.

## SEO mechanics

- Include the target keyword naturally in the title.
- Include it in the meta description.
- Include it in the H1.
- Include it within the first 100 words.
- Use it or a close variation in at least one H2 when natural.
- Keep the meta title at or below 60 characters.
- Keep the meta description at or below 155 characters.
- Write the meta description to earn a click, not only to include keywords.
- Match the article to the search intent found in the SERP.
- Avoid unnatural keyword repetition.
- Use related terms only where they improve clarity.
- Use natural internal-link anchor text.
- Add descriptive alt text to every referenced image.
- Alt text must describe the actual image, not repeat the target keyword unnecessarily.
- Do not create multiple articles targeting the same intent without a clear differentiation.
- Flag possible keyword cannibalization before drafting.

## Research and sourcing

Use reliable sources for claims about:

- Shopify behavior.
- Google Search.
- Structured data.
- Indexing.
- Technical SEO.
- AI discovery.
- Accessibility.
- Industry statistics.

Prefer:

1. Official Shopify documentation.
2. Google Search Central.
3. Schema.org.
4. Official platform or product documentation.
5. Original research and reputable industry publications.

Community discussions, Reddit posts, and merchant reviews can be used to understand real-world experiences, but they should not be treated as definitive technical evidence.

Do not cite one competitor’s marketing page as proof that its method improves rankings.

## Writing rules

- Use American English unless the content brief specifies another locale.
- Prefer active voice.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Explain acronyms the first time they appear.
- Use lists only when they improve scanning.
- Avoid unnecessary repetition.
- Avoid filler conclusions that merely restate the introduction.
- Use tables only for genuine comparisons.
- Do not write content that feels generated from a fixed template.
- Vary examples, transitions, and article flow while preserving the required structure.

## Things to never do

- Never fabricate statistics, studies, quotes, reviews, customers, or user counts.
- Never invent Shopify features, settings, limitations, or APIs.
- Never claim that SEO results are guaranteed.
- Never claim that a page will rank first.
- Never promise instant indexing.
- Never promise ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or another AI will cite or recommend a store.
- Never present correlation as causation.
- Never reproduce copyrighted articles, product descriptions, or long quotations.
- Never copy a competitor’s marketing text.
- Never claim Zion has a feature that is not available.
- Never publish internal URLs that are not listed in the pipeline configuration.
- Never recommend keyword stuffing, hidden text, doorway pages, fake reviews, link schemes, or other manipulative practices.
- Never use “seamless,” “revolutionize,” “skyrocket,” “dominate,” or “game-changing.”
